The Mission: Geo-Consulting

Scenario: You are a geological consultant hired to analyze a specific region of the world. Your client needs to understand the fundamental nature of the ground before they can invest in agriculture or infrastructure projects there.

The Question: How does the specific geology of your region dictate the lifestyle, food, and safety of the people living there?

Deliverable: You will produce a digital report (PDF or Slide Deck) covering the three pillars of investigation listed below.

Phase 1: Choose Your Region

Select one of the following regions. No two students in a breakout group may have the same region.

Great Plains, USA

Soil Type: Mollisols

The "Breadbasket of the World." Rich, dark soils formed by grasslands.

Amazon Basin, Brazil

Soil Type: Oxisols

Ancient, highly weathered red soils. High iron, low nutrients.

Sicily, Italy

Soil Type: Andisols

Soils born from fire. Volcanic ash, highly porous and fertile.

The Sahel, Africa

Soil Type: Aridisols

Desert soils. Dry, salty, and facing the challenge of desertification.

Peatlands, Ireland

Soil Type: Histosols

Wet, organic soils made of decomposing plant matter. High carbon.

Loess Plateau, China

Soil Type: Entisols

Wind-blown silt deposits. Highly erodible but historically significant.

Phase 2: The Three Pillars of Investigation

Your report must address these three specific areas of study:

1. The Geology (The "What")

Identify the scientific classification and composition.

  • Taxonomy: What is the official Soil Order name?
  • CLORPT: Which factor (Climate, Organisms, Relief, Parent Material, Time) was the most dominant in creating this soil?
  • Profile: Describe the horizons. What color is it and why? (e.g., Red = Iron).

2. The Harvest (The "Food")

Analyze the agricultural economy.

  • Native Crops: What grows best here naturally?
  • Deficiencies: What nutrients is the soil missing? Do farmers need specific fertilizers?
  • The Meal: Propose a "Dinner Plate" containing only foods that can be grown in this specific soil.

3. The Infrastructure (The "Build")

Evaluate engineering constraints.

  • Stability: Is the soil prone to "Shrink-Swell" (cracking foundations) or Liquefaction (earthquakes)?
  • Materials: How have traditional cultures used this soil to build? (e.g., Adobe, brick, stilts).
  • Hazards: Are there sinkholes, landslides, or erosion risks?

Resources

Use these tools to gather your data:

  • USDA Web Soil Survey (For US Regions)
  • ISRIC - World Soil Information (Global Data)
  • SoilWeb Earth

Phase 3: Grading Rubric

Total Points: 100

Criteria Excellent (25 pts) Proficient (20 pts) Developing (15 pts) Incomplete (10 pts)
Geological Accuracy Correctly identifies Taxonomy, Formation Factors, and Horizon details with scientific depth. Identifies Taxonomy and Factors but lacks detail on Horizons or specific composition. General description of soil provided, but lacks scientific terminology or Taxonomy. Incorrect soil identification or missing major geological concepts.
Agricultural Analysis Connects soil chemistry directly to crop success/failure. Creative "Dinner Plate" example. Lists crops grown in the region but lacks explanation of why the soil supports them. Lists crops that do not actually grow in that region or misses the soil connection. Section missing or lists generic foods only.
Engineering & Infrastructure Detailed analysis of hazards (liquefaction, erosion) and historical building methods. Mentions building materials but misses the geological hazard aspect. Vague reference to buildings (e.g., "houses are made of wood") without soil context. Section missing.
Visuals & Professionalism Report is visually stunning, uses maps, diagrams, and is cited correctly. Report is clean and organized, includes some images. Minor citation errors. Report is text-heavy, disorganized, or lacks necessary maps/images. Formatting makes the report difficult to read. No citations.
